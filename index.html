    Best Practice — ATO Substantiation

    • Keep receipts for 5 years — required by the ATO for any deduction over $300 total work expenses.
    • Log your WFH hours at the time — the ATO will not accept retrospective estimates. Use a diary, timesheet, or roster.
    • Don't double-dip — if you use the fixed-rate method (70¢/hr) you can't also claim phone, internet, electricity separately.
    • Separate work and personal — bank statement is good evidence; using a dedicated card for work purchases simplifies audit.
    • Depreciate assets > $300 — laptops, monitors, office furniture are depreciated unless under the $300 immediate-deduction threshold.
    • Spouse income splitting — keep investment income in the lower-earning spouse's name where legitimate; offset eligibility differs.
